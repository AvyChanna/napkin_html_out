The equality case is trickier.

The easier direction is when K is a splitting field. Assume K = F(α1,…,αn) is the splitting field of some separable polynomial p ∈ F[x] with n distinct roots α1,…,αn. Adjoin them one by one:

Similarly, given a choice of τ1, there are [F(α1,α2) : F(α1)] ways to pick τ2. Multiplying these all together gives the desired [K : F].

Now assume K∕F is Galois. First, we state:

Lemma 52.8.3
Let K∕F be Galois, and p ∈ F[x] irreducible. If any root of p (in F) lies in K, then all of them do, and in fact p is separable.
