46.3  Number fields

Prototypical example for this section: ℚ() is a typical number field.

Given any algebraic number α, we’re able to consider fields of the form ℚ(α). Let us write down the more full version.

Definition 46.3.1. A number field K is a field containing ℚ as a subfield which is a finite-dimensional ℚ-vector space. The degree of K is its dimension.

Example 46.3.4 (Adjoining an algebraic number)
Let α be the root of some irreducible polynomial P(x) in ℚ. The field ℚ(α) is a field extension as well, and the basis is 1,α,α2,…,αm−1, where m is the degree of α. In particular, the degree of ℚ(α) is just the degree of α.

Example 46.3.5 (Non-examples of number fields)
ℝ and ℂ are not number fields since there is no finite ℚ-basis of them.
