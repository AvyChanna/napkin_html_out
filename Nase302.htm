48.2  Ideal arithmetic

Prototypical example for this section: (x)(y) = (xy). In any case, think in terms of generators.

Finally, since we want to do factorization we better have some notion of divisibility. So we define:

Definition 48.2.4. We say 𝔞 divides 𝔟 and write 𝔞∣𝔟 if 𝔞 ⊇𝔟.

Note the reversal of inclusions! So (3) divides (15), because (15) is contained in (3); every multiple of 15 is a multiple of 3. And from the example in the previous section: In ℤ[], (3,1 −) divides (3) and (1 −).
