Problem 35B (The insane scientist). Let Ω = ℝ2, and let ℰ be the set of (non-degenerate) rectangles. Let ρ(E) = 1 for every rectangle E ∈ℰ. Ignoring my advice, the insane scientist uses ρ to construct an outer measure μ∗, as in ?? .

(a)
Find μ∗(S) for each subset S of ℝ2.
(b)
Which sets are μ∗-measurable?

You should find that no rectangle is μ∗-measurable, unsurprisingly foiling the scientist.
