42.2  The projection principle

Before proceeding I need to say something really important.

Theorem 42.2.1 (Projection principle)
 Let U be an open subset of the vector space V . Let W be an n-dimensional real vector space with basis w1,…,wn. Then there is a bijection between continuous functions f : U → W and n-tuples of continuous f1,f2,…,fn : U → ℝ by projection onto the ith basis element, i.e. 

Proof. Obvious. □

The theorem remains true if one replaces “continuous” by “differentiable”, “smooth”, “arbitrary”, or most other reasonable words. Translation:

To think about a function f : U → ℝn, it suffices to think about each coordinate separately.

For this reason, we’ll most often be interested in functions f : U → ℝ. That’s why the dual space V ∨ is so important.
