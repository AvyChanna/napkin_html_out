Definition 13.3.3. An orthonormal basis of a finite-dimensional inner product space V is a basis e1, …, en such that = 1 for every i and = 0 for any i≠j.

Example 13.3.4 (ℝn and ℂn have standard bases)
In ℝn and ℂn equipped with the standard dot product, the standard basis e1, …, en is also orthonormal.

This is no loss of generality:

Theorem 13.3.5 (Gram-Schmidt)
Let V be a finite-dimensional inner product space. Then it has an orthonormal basis.
