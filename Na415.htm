1 This can be thought of as analogous to the way that we think of a group as an abstract object in its own right, even though Cayley’s Theorem tells us that any group is a subgroup of the permutation group. Note this wasn’t always the case! During the 19th century, a group was literally defined as a subset of GL(n) or of Sn. In fact Sylow developed his theorems without the word “group” Only much later did the abstract definition of a group was given, an abstract set G which was independent of any embedding into Sn, and an object in its own right.
