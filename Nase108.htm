13.5  A few harder problems to think about

Problem 13A (Pythagorean theorem). Show that if = 0 in an inner product space, then 2 + 2 = 2.

Problem 13B⋆ (Finite-dimensional Hilbert). Show that a finite-dimensional inner product space is a Hilbert space.

Problem 13C (Taiwan IMO camp).       In a town there are n people and k clubs. Each club has an odd number of members, and any two clubs have an even number of common members. Prove that k ≤ n.

Problem 13D⋆ (Inner product structure of tensors). Let V and W be finite-dimensional inner product spaces over k, where k is either ℝ or ℂ.

(a)
Find a canonical way to make V ⊗kW into an inner product space too.
(b)
Let e1, …, en be an orthonormal basis of V and f1, …, fm be an orthonormal basis of W. What’s an orthonormal basis of V ⊗ W?

Problem 13E (Putnam 2014).       Let n be a positive integer. What is the largest k for which there exist n×n matrices M1,…,Mk and N1,…,Nk with real entries such that for all i and j, the matrix product MiNj has a zero entry somewhere on its diagonal if and only if i≠j?
