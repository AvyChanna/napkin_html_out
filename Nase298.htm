Now we can state the theorem.

Theorem 47.2.11 (Algebraic integers are closed under + and ×)
The set ℤ is closed under addition and multiplication; i.e. it is a ring. In particular, 𝒪K is also a ring for any number field K.

Proof. Let α,β ∈ ℤ. Then ℤ[α] and ℤ[β] are finitely generated. Hence so is ℤ[α,β]. (Details: if ℤ[α] has ℤ-basis a1,…,am and ℤ[β] has ℤ-basis b1,…,bn, then take the mn elements aibj.)

Now ℤ[α±β] and ℤ[αβ] are subsets of ℤ[α,β] and so they are also finitely generated. Hence α ± β and αβ are algebraic integers. □
